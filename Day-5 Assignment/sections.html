Current Affairs



1. India Hosts Major Naval Gathering

The Indian Navy recently hosted the International Fleet Review 2026 in Visakhapatnam. Naval ships, aircraft, and personnel from dozens of countries participated. The event focused on strengthening maritime cooperation, improving naval coordination, and promoting security in the Indian Ocean region. It was one of the largest international naval gatherings ever hosted by India.

2. Ukraine-Russia War Escalates Further

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced that Ukraine would intensify its efforts against Russian military targets after a series of drone attacks near Moscow. The conflict continues to affect regional security, global energy markets, and international diplomacy. European countries are also dealing with related economic and humanitarian challenges.

Switzerland Rejects Population Cap Proposal

Swiss voters rejected a proposal that would have capped the country's population at 10 million people by 2050. Supporters argued it would help manage resources and infrastructure, while opponents said immigration is important for economic growth and addressing labor shortages. The referendum result is being viewed as a significant development in the ongoing global debate over immigration policy.


Politics



1. U.S. Political Tensions Over Voting Laws and Housing Bill

A major political controversy erupted in the United States after President Donald Trump postponed the signing of a bipartisan housing affordability bill. The administration instead focused on pushing the "SAVE America Act," which includes stricter voter identification and citizenship verification requirements. At the same time, a federal judge blocked key parts of an executive order that required documentary proof of citizenship for voting, ruling that election laws are primarily the responsibility of Congress and state governments. The developments have sparked intense debate between Republicans and Democrats ahead of upcoming elections.

2. Leadership Change Looms in the United Kingdom

The United Kingdom is experiencing significant political uncertainty following the resignation announcement of Prime Minister Keir Starmer. Andy Burnham, currently one of the leading figures in the Labour Party, has emerged as the frontrunner to become the next Prime Minister. Political analysts view this as an important moment for British politics as the country continues to deal with economic challenges, post-Brexit issues, and foreign policy concerns related to Ukraine and European security.

3. South Korea Marks One Year Under President Lee Jae Myung

South Korean President Lee Jae Myung completed his first year in office after a turbulent period in the country's politics. His administration has focused on economic recovery, political reforms, and strengthening South Korea's international position. Despite facing opposition criticism and economic pressures, Lee continues to maintain significant public support while pursuing his policy agenda. Political observers consider his first year crucial in shaping South Korea's future domestic and foreign policies
